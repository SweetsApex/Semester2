The Eastern Cottontail Rabbit

One week to three week old kit!

Physical Identifiers:

These creatures are often identified through a white spot on their foreheads, they're known to be red-brown or grey-brown in colour, with
large hind legs, large ears, and a short, fluffy-white tail. Their underside fur is often white, with a rusty patch on their tails.

Common Habitat:

Rabbit in its natural habitat:
  • The Eastern Cottontail Rabbits are often found across the United States of America, southern Canada, Mexico, Central America, or northern South America.
  • These rabbits are known to thrive in habitats such as open fields that are grassy, with supporting shrubbery / foliage for optimal survival.
  • Males often occupy more land across their lifetimes (1.4 acres), versus their female counterparts (1.2 acres).
  • As to be expected, breeding season greatly affects male movement throughout their lifetimes as they seek suitable mates.

Survivability:

The Cottontail with its winter coat!
  • The Eastern Cottontail Rabbits often have a dark brown coat during the winter months. To optimally thrive throughout the winter, they will often stay in areas known to them with good vegetation in summer. Meaning they'll stay in known areas with lots of shrubbery, wood areas with good cover, dens / small holes, and especially shrubbery with surrounding walls / stones, pertaining to rabbits that live near cities.
  • Ironically, these animals do not dig their own dens, they only situate their own nests for nursing.
  • During the mating season, males will often fight one another to "win" a possible mate!
  • The average Eastern Cottontail Rabbits lifespan is approximately 15 months in the wild, while the longest recorded living was about 5 years.
  • These little sprinters can hop! They often top-out at 29km/h! While escaping a predator at their top speeds, they'll often maneuver left and right to throw off their scent trails.
  • They're known to be creatures of prey, meaning they're more often the animals being hunted rather than the hunter! Due to their place on the food chain being lower, they are known to be herbivores, primarily consuming local vegetation.
  • Despite being herbivores, in extreme cases, some rabbits have been known to consume arthropods which have been found in their pellets before!
  • Regardless of their place on the food chain, these little guys are territorial! They will often fight other animals, including predators if they feel they have no other options with their powerful hind legs!
  • Local threats can easily be sniffed out as these rabbits have up to 100 million scent receptors and can twitch their nose up to 120 times per minute!

In Conclusion:

Eastern Cottontail Rabbits are very beautiful creatures, although they can be menacing when provoked. Thriving across North America, indulging
on local foliage and travelling minimally unless to seek food or a better mate, these little guys were a wonderful choice for this project
